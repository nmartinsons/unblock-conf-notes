UN:BLOCK Conference — Key Insights

April 1–2, 2026 · Riga

Stablecoins

Growth potential

~$200B in stablecoins vs $20T+ U.S. M2 money supply — the market is still in its infancy.

EUR vs USD

EUR/USD stablecoin ratio stands at roughly 5/95. EUR stablecoins are growing, but slowly.

Localised use cases are critical to close this gap.

Distribution is the problem

Issuance is not the challenge — getting stablecoins into everyday hands is. First users will be institutions.

Business idea

The winning product will make stablecoins invisible — users should not feel like they are using crypto at all.

Agentic payments

Stablecoins + smart contracts = programmable, autonomous payments. Faster, transparent, and safer than traditional rails.

Data risk and loss of control are the key concerns.

Regulation & MiCA

Competitive advantage

MiCA can be a bridge between the EU and regions like Japan — a trust signal, not just a compliance burden.

Cost vs benefit

MiCA is expensive in time and resources. Companies without strong governance knowledge will struggle to obtain a licence.

Stablecoin licensing

Stablecoins require an EMI licence in the EU — not MiCA. Regulators are still navigating the line between CBDCs and stablecoins.

USA clarity

The Genius Act requires stablecoins to be backed by T-bills. If the Clarity Act passes, it will have major global repercussions.

Real-world assets & tokenisation

Legal bottleneck

The legal side is the main pain point for RWAs — resolving it unlocks foreign investment and partial ownership.

Scale of opportunity

By 2035, trillions of dollars are expected to move on-chain through tokenisation.

Ownership nuance

Most tokenised products are derivatives — holders do not actually own the underlying asset. Tokenised stocks offer price appreciation, not dividends.

Real estate

Adding stablecoins to real estate escrow speeds up transactions — funds are held on-chain until sale conditions are met.

Latvia & local landscape

Cautious investors

Latvians are dipping their toes — investing small sums up to €1k. Older demographics are adopting crypto faster than younger ones.

Education gap

No central hub exists for crypto, EMI, and legal knowledge in Latvia. The gap is both a problem and an opportunity.

Demographics

Female investors represent 41% of all crypto holders in Latvia — a higher share than many expect.

Policy direction

The capital gains tax increase in Latvia was widely seen as a mistake and may be cancelled or reduced.

Adoption & education

Mindset shift

You cannot educate people who do not want to learn. The focus must be on translating meaning and providing clear personal value.

SEPA barrier

EU stablecoin adoption is harder because SEPA payments are already instant. The incentive to switch is low for everyday users.

Risk management

The 1% rule — never risk more than 1% of your portfolio on a single trade.
